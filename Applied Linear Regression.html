Applied Linear Regression on Auto Data Set

Project Overview

This project explores the relationship between vehicle characteristics and fuel efficiency (mpg) using Python and Statsmodels. Both simple and multiple linear regression models were applied to the Auto dataset to analyze predictor effects, interpret coefficients, and visualize trends.

We modeled mpg as a function of all other variables except name. Key insights include:

Reflective Summary

Working on this Applied Linear Regression project gave me hands-on experience in analyzing real-world data and interpreting statistical models. By performing both simple and multiple linear regressions on the Auto dataset, I learned how vehicle characteristics like horsepower, weight, and year influence fuel efficiency (mpg).

The diagnostic plots were especially insightful, showing me the importance of checking assumptions such as linearity, homoscedasticity, and identifying outliers or high-leverage points. I saw firsthand how residual patterns can indicate potential problems in the model and why transformations or interaction terms may improve model fit.

One challenge I faced was handling the dataset to ensure all variables were numeric and correctly formatted for regression. I overcame this by carefully converting categorical variables and checking for missing values, which reinforced the importance of clean and structured data before analysis.

Another challenge was interpreting multiple regression results, including coefficients and their statistical significance. Through the ANOVA tests and correlation matrix, I gained a deeper understanding of how predictors work together and how to interpret the impact of each variable on mpg.

Overall, this project enhanced my technical skills in Python, Statsmodels, and data visualization, and strengthened my ability to communicate statistical findings clearly. It also reinforced the importance of systematic planning, careful coding, and thoughtful reflection when working with data-driven conclusions.
